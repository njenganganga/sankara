THOMAS SANKARA

Thomas Isidore Noël Sankara was born in 21 December 1949 as the third of 10 children to Joseph and Marguerite Sankara. He was a Burkinabe military officer, Marxists revolutionary and Pan-Africanist who served as president of Burkina Faso from his coup in 1983 to his assassination in 1987. He is viewed by his supporters as a charismatic and iconic figure of revolution.




EARLY LIFE


He who feeds you controls you!

-He spent his early years in Gaoua, a town in the humid southwest to which his father was transferred as an auxiliary gendarme.

-As the son of one of the few African functionaries then employed by the colonial state, he enjoyed a relatively privileged position.

-The family lived in a brick house with the families of other gendarmes at the top of a hill overlooking the rest of Gaoua.

-Sankara attended primary school at Bobo-Dioulasso. He applied himself seriously to his schoolwork and excelled in mathematics and French.

-Sankara decided to keep on his education at the neareest lycee Quezzin Coulibaly proved to be a turning point. This step got him out of his father's household since the lycée was in Bobo-Dioulasso, the country's commercial centre.At the lycée, Sankara made close friends, including Fidèle Too, whom he later named a minister in his government; and Soumane Touré, who was in a more advanced class.

-Sankara was a decent guitarist. He played in a band named Tout-a-Coup Jazz and rode a bicycle.



MILITARY CAREER


Anything that man can imagine, he is capable of creating!

After basic military training in secondary school in 1966, Sankara began his military career at the age of 19 and a year later was sent to Madagascar for officer training at Antisirabe where he witnessed popular uprisings in 1971 and 1972 against the government of Philibert Tsiranana and first read the works of Karl Max and Vladimir Lenin, which profoundly influenced his political views for the rest of his life.

Returning to Upper Volta in 1972, he fought in a border war between Upper Volta and Mali by 1974. He earned fame for his performance in the conflict, but years later would renounce the fighting as 'useless and unjust', a reflection of his growing political consciousness. He also became a popular figure in the capital of Ouagadougou.



PRESIDENCY


He who does not feed you can demand nothing of you!

A Coup d’etat organized by Blaise Compaoré made Sankara President on 4 August 1983 at the age of 33. The coup d'état was supported by Libya, which was at the time on the verge of war with France in Chad.

As President, he promoted the 'Democratic and Popular Revolution. The ideology of the Revolution was defined by Sankara as anti-imperialist in a speech on 2 October 1983.His policy was oriented toward fighting corruption and promoting reforestation.

On 4 August 1984, the first anniversary of his accession, he renamed the country Burkina Faso, meaning 'the land of upright people'. He also gave it a new flag and wrote a new national anthem.



ASSASSINATION


You cannot carry out fundamental change without a certain amount of madness!

On 15 October 1987, Sankara was killed by an armed group with twelve other officials in a coup d’etat organized by his former colleague Blaise Compaore. When accounting for his overthrow, Compaoré stated that Sankara jeopardized foreign relations with former colonial power France and neighbouring Ivory Coast, and accused his former comrade of plotting to assassinate opponents.

According to Halouna Traoré, the sole survivor of Sankara's assassination, Sankara was attending a meeting with the Conseil de l'Entente. His assassins singled out Sankara and executed him. He was quickly buried in an unmarked grave while his widow Mariam and two children fled the nation.
